# Wave 1A HeyReach Copy — Mid-Market Provider Groups (Subtier 1A)

**Audience:** Directors/VPs at multi-site primary care / specialty medical groups (~50–300 clinicians), not IDNs, not payers, not vendors.

**Guardrails (all touches):**
- Product is **clinical decision support (CDS)** — not autonomous diagnosis; align with public/legal framing.
- No unsourced ROI % or revenue promises unless account grade allows numerical claims per [[COCM_CONFIDENCE_GRADE_RULES]].
- Pain anchors: BH workload in primary care, PHQ-9 / care-manager documentation load, CoCM / VBC alignment where relevant (discovery, not a claim).

**Character targets:** Connection note ≤200 characters. First DM ~600–800 characters, soft CTA (e.g. 15-min call), not a calendar link.

---

## 1. Operational Owner

**Primary theme:** Workflow / ops.

### Connection request (≤200 chars)

> Multisite group + BH in primary care is a lot to operationalize—curious if care-manager doc load is a theme on your side. Open to connect?

_(199 chars)_

### First DM

> Thanks for connecting. I work with medical groups on how care teams document and act on depression / anxiety in the primary-care workflow—without adding another “side system” for managers to chase.
>
> A lot of groups your size are trying to get PHQ-9 / follow-up work to actually close the loop for the PCP, not just check a box. We’re not pitching a new care-management product; it’s decision support that sits in the existing visit and handoff path.
>
> If any of that sounds familiar, happy to do a 15-min pass—no deck required unless you want one.

---

## 2. Clinical Champion

**Primary theme:** Clinical outcomes / safety / credibility.

### Connection request

> Saw you’re leading clinical direction at a medical group—interested in how groups are handling BH in primary care with limited psych access. Worth a connect?

_(198 chars)_

### First DM

> Appreciate the add. I’m focused on how PCPs get **defensible, instrument-backed** context for depression / anxiety in the visit—so the story holds up in med staff / quality convos, not just the sales one.
>
> The angle I care about: transparent methodology, bounded outputs, and no “autonomous diagnosis” positioning—CDS that the medical staff can stand behind.
>
> If you’re the right person to pressure-test that with, I’d value 15 minutes. If not, a pointer to who owns ambulatory BH clinically would help.

---

## 3. Economic Buyer

**Primary theme:** ROI / revenue — **do not lead cold** per sequencing; use only after warm signal at same account.

### Connection request

> Leading finance/strategy at a growing medical group—seen a few peers rethink BH-related reimbursement hygiene (e.g. CCM/BHI adjacent work). Open to connect?

_(195 chars)_

### First DM

> Thanks for connecting. I’ll be direct: this shouldn’t be a cold ROI spam thread.
>
> Groups like yours often leave behavioral-health-related **workflow revenue** under-captured because documentation and eligibility work sits between front desk, nursing, and PCP—and nobody owns the whole chain.
>
> I’m not asking for a pricing conversation here—only whether your group already has a crisp owner for “BH + primary care economics,” or if that’s still split across ops and finance. If there’s a warm thread from your ops/clinical side already, happy to align with that.

_(Short enough for first touch after warm intro—adjust if truly cold.)_

## 4. Technical Gatekeeper

**Primary theme:** Integration / security / EHR reality.

### Connection request

> Health IT + ambulatory workflows—curious how your group evaluates new clinical modules without blowing Epic/Athena change windows. Connect?

_(178 chars)_

### First DM

> Thanks for the connection. I know your inbox is full of vendors promising FHIR fairy tales.
>
> Practical question for medical-group stacks: when clinical asks for “BH support in the PCP workflow,” how do you keep it inside existing auth, audit, and release governance—without another bolt-on that dies in security review?
>
> We’re biased toward minimal integration surface area and clear CDS boundaries. If that’s the bar on your side, I’d take 15 minutes to compare notes—no implementation deep-dive unless you want it.

## 5. BH / Quality Influencer

**Primary theme:** Quality / compliance — **demo-stage** influencer per sequencing; lighter outbound volume.

### Connection request

> Behavioral health + quality measurement at a medical group is a thin intersection—would love to compare notes on follow-up / measurement realities.

_(163 chars)_

### First DM

> Thanks for connecting. Teams in your lane often own the uncomfortable overlap: **quality metrics** that touch depression follow-up, and the reality that primary care doesn’t have psych capacity to absorb referrals.
>
> I’m interested in whether your group measures “closure” (follow-up completed, documented handoff) versus “screening rates” alone—and where the workflow usually breaks.
>
> Happy to keep this exploratory unless you’re actively evaluating tools; if a demo ever makes sense, we’d want ops + clinical in the room anyway.
