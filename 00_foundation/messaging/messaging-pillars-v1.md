# Messaging pillars (v1)

**Layer 2 —** links to Layer 1 nodes. All external copy must pass legal/clinical review.

## Pillar A — Primary care reality

**Idea:** Mental health workload lands on PCPs; tools must fit how they already work.

- **Anchor nodes:** [[PRIMARY_CARE_WEDGE_ICP]], [[PUBLIC_VALUE_PROPOSITION_HOME]]
- **Talk track:** Volume + responsibility in primary care; need workflow-native support, not parallel paper processes.
- **Avoid:** Implying PCPs are “failing” patients; blaming clinicians.

## Pillar B — Workflow integration

**Idea:** Screen and document within the **EHR context** you already use.

- **Anchor nodes:** [[EHR_INTEGRATION_SMART_ON_FHIR]], [[B2B_CLINIC_BUYER_MODEL]]
- **Talk track:** SMART on FHIR / HL7-class integrations; Epic MyChart onboarding story where relevant.
- **Avoid:** Promising unsupported EHRs or write-back depth without SE confirmation.

## Pillar C — Clinician-led decisions (CDS)

**Idea:** AI assists; **clinicians decide**.

- **Anchor nodes:** [[CDS_NOT_DIAGNOSIS_FRAMING]], [[PRIVACY_AND_HIPAA_ROLE]]
- **Talk track:** Outputs support diagnosis/treatment decisions; organizational governance over what patients see.
- **Avoid:** “Autonomous diagnosis,” “replacing the clinician,” or chatbot-as-doctor framing.

## Pillar D — Safety grounded in policy

**Idea:** Risk signals route to **care teams** with clear limits.

- **Anchor nodes:** [[PATIENT_APP_SAFETY_ALERTS]]
- **Talk track:** Clinic-configured notifications; crisis resource emails are **not** emergency services.
- **Avoid:** “We monitor you 24/7” or instant emergency response claims.

## Pillar E — Evidence humility

**Idea:** We cite **standard instruments** transparently; we **do not** confuse marketing charts with peer-reviewed product outcomes.

- **Anchor nodes:** [[CLINICAL_INSTRUMENTS_SURFACE]], [[COMPETITIVE_EVIDENCE_GAP]], [[EVIDENCE_DISCIPLINE_RESEARCH_SYNTHESIS]]
- **Talk track:** Third-party scale validity vs product roadmap for validation studies.
- **Avoid:** Quoting homepage animation percentages in diligence without approved studies.

## “Never say” list (draft)

- Anything that contradicts [[CDS_NOT_DIAGNOSIS_FRAMING]] or [[PATIENT_APP_SAFETY_ALERTS]].
- Absolute superiority claims vs unnamed competitors without substantiation.
- HIPAA “certification” language (unless counsel approves exact phrasing).
