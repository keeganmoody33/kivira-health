## Tone check — what to keep, what to avoid

**Keep:**
- "Quick context" + reason for reaching out — disarms the "what is this" reflex.
- "Most useful thing I can do this quarter is learn from…" — frames the ask as your problem, not theirs.
- "No pitch — just curious whether…" — explicitly disclaims a sales motion.
- "What you'd push back on" — invites honest critique, which warm operators actually respond to.

**Avoid (rewrite if you find yourself drifting here):**
- Any version of "we help X save Y%" — that's a pitch.
- Mentioning the product name beyond "Kivira Health" — keep this email about *them* and the question, not a product walkthrough.
- "Calendly link" or "book time" — a Calendly link reads as automated cadence. Better: wait for a yes, then propose two specific times.
- Subtier labels ("1C", "Tier 2A") — these are internal. Never send them.
- Anything that implies they specifically were targeted — say "places like yours," not "you fit our buyer persona."
