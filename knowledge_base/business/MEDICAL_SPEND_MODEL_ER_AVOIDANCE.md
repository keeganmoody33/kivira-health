# Medical Spend Model (ER Avoidance)

The Medical Spend Model positions Kivira as a cost-avoidance tool for full-risk VBC groups and health systems. The core thesis: untreated mental health is a primary driver of "Frequent Flyer" ER visits and costly psychiatric boarding situations.

[INFERRED: Gemini directional analysis - verify before external use]

## Key Points

- **Target buyer:** Full-risk VBC groups, Health Systems, ACOs
- **Value driver:** Reducing avoidable psychiatric ER visits and mental health boarding
- **Mechanism:** Earlier identification and intervention via Kivira screening in primary care
- **Timeline:** Year 3+ play once sufficient data exists to prove cost curve bending

## The ROI Logic

The directional formula:

> (Annual Psych ER Visits) x (Estimated % reduction via early intervention) x (Avg. ER visit cost) = Direct Cost Savings

Psychiatric ER visits and "boarding" (patients waiting for psych beds) represent significant costs. Standard BHI impact studies suggest meaningful reduction potential through integrated behavioral health screening.

## Connection to Total Cost of Care

By Year 3-5, this model supports the "Medical Spend Waterfall" narrative:
- Patients with untreated BH conditions have higher ER visit rates
- Early identification keeps patients in clinic settings vs. high-cost hospital settings
- Data accumulation proves Kivira "bends the cost curve"
