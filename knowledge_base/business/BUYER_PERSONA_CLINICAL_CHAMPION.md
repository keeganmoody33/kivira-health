# Buyer Persona: The Clinical Champion

**Archetype:** Dr. Jennifer R. — Medical Director, Integrated Behavioral Health

[VERIFIED: Pattern synthesized from 10+ LinkedIn profiles, AHRQ integrated care literature, PHTI 2025 purchasing survey]

## Professional Snapshot

Board-certified family medicine physician with 8–12 years clinical experience who transitioned into a dual role: ~50% direct patient care, ~50% leading the health system's integrated behavioral health (IBH) program. Career arc: clinician → IBH champion → program founder → director.

**Credentials pattern:**
- Fellowship in primary care psychiatry or addiction medicine
- AAFP and CFHA (Collaborative Family Healthcare Association) membership
- AHRQ Integration Academy training or SAMHSA PBHCI conference attendance

**Origin story:** Typically entered leadership after a moment of clinical frustration—a patient with untreated depression who fell through the cracks, or PHQ-9 scores collected and promptly ignored. Built a CoCM program from scratch: recruiting BH care managers, finding psychiatric consultants, designing clinical workflows, figuring out CPT 99492–99494 billing with revenue cycle.

**Compensation:** $250k–$350k (dual clinical + administrative)

## Daily Reality

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 7:30 AM | Reviews overnight patient messages, flag escalations from BH care managers |
| 8:00–12:00 | Sees patients in family medicine clinic; MH concerns arise in ~3 of 5 visits |
| 12:30 PM | Weekly IBH team huddle—reviewing CoCM patient registry, discussing non-improvers |
| 2:00 PM | Performance improvement meeting—explaining why PHQ-9 follow-up completion is below 75% benchmark |
| 3:30 PM | Vendor demo call—four tabs open including Epic, testing whether tool output feels like clinical aid or liability |
| 5:00 PM | Reviews IRB amendment for QI project—CFO keeps asking for peer-reviewed proof before expanding IBH budget |

**Her KPIs:**
- Depression remission rate (PHQ-9 ≥50% reduction)
- CoCM registry enrollment and engagement rate
- Psychiatric consultation response time
- Medication initiation rate for screen-positive patients
- Clinician satisfaction with BH workflow support

**Time pressure reality:** She is simultaneously physician, program manager, supervisor, compliance monitor, and grant writer. No discretionary hours. Any new tool must subtract work from her day, not add to it.

## Decision Driver Analysis

Dr. Jennifer is a **"trust first" buyer**. She will not champion a tool to her CMO and CMIO unless she has personally been convinced it is clinically sound.

**Validation process:**
1. **Evidence gatekeeping:** Looks for validated instruments (PHQ-9, GAD-7, MINI), peer-reviewed references, external validation. Immediately skeptical of "AI-powered diagnosis"—prefers "clinical decision support." Kivira's CDS-only framing is exactly right.
2. **Workflow simulation:** Will ask for live demo in simulated Epic environment. Will deliberately try to break it—entering ambiguous or risk-elevated responses to test safety notification pathway.
3. **Peer validation:** Will call 2–3 colleagues at other systems who have piloted the tool. LinkedIn and CFHA listservs are her informal reference network. A call from a peer at Wellstar would be highly persuasive.
4. **Safety review:** Will scrutinize high-risk patient alert workflow. Does the alert go to the right person? What is the SLA for response? Requires documentation.
5. **IRB/QI pathway:** Wants to know if she can publish a pilot outcomes paper. Publishable evidence from her program serves both her career and her organization's credibility.

**Risk tolerance:** Low on patient safety; moderate on technology adoption; high on workflow innovation if evidence supports it.

**ROI framing she responds to:** Clinical quality first, not revenue. She wants "diagnostic concordance improved by X%," "follow-up rate increased by Y%," "time from screen to treatment plan reduced by Z days." Financial arguments are secondary.

## Communication Profile

**Preferred channels:** Direct email for substantive conversations; LinkedIn for credibility-checking vendors before meetings; phone for trusted peers

**Content that earns trust:** Case studies with specific clinical outcomes (not just "improved satisfaction"), peer-reviewed citations, conference presentations by clinicians she respects

**Red flags:** Marketing-heavy language ("revolutionary," "game-changing"), claims about AI accuracy without transparent methodology, any framing that positions tool as replacing clinician judgment

**Meeting preference:** 30-minute working demos over 60-minute pitch decks; she wants to navigate the interface herself

**Response time:** Appreciates follow-up within 24 hours but will not respond under pressure; aggressive cadence will slow her down

**Best time to reach:** Early morning (7–8 AM) or late afternoon (after 4 PM); unavailable mid-morning and at lunch

## Professional Motivation Map

**Career driver:** Wants to be recognized as a national model-builder for integrated behavioral health—the person who figured out how to make CoCM actually work at scale. Publication in Annals of Family Medicine or Health Affairs would be a career milestone.

**Recognition currency:** Peer recognition > financial incentives. Wants invitations to speak at AAFP or CFHA conferences and to be cited by colleagues as the clinical lead who built something replicable.

**Fear that shapes decisions:** Missing a high-risk patient. A false negative on suicidal ideation in a tool she championed would be professionally and personally devastating. This is why she reads every line of a vendor's safety documentation.

**Personal values:** Evidence-based medicine, equity (acutely aware that integrated care access is unevenly distributed), and the principle that primary care should be the front door for mental health.

## Trigger to Yes

**Strongest proof point:** Peer reference call from a family medicine director at a live Kivira site (e.g., Wellstar)

**Fastest path to champion:** Invite to co-author a pilot outcomes paper

**Most common deal-killer:** Safety workflow ambiguity
