# Existential Data Point: CoCM Revenue Realization Rate

An **Existential Data Point (EDP)** is the critical metric that creates genuine urgency and transforms a nice-to-have solution into a must-have requirement. It's the data point so crucial to a business that it can determine success or failure.

[INFERRED: Framework application from SaaS EDP methodology to Kivira market context]

## The Winning EDP: CoCM Revenue Realization Rate

**Definition:** The percentage of eligible mental health encounters in a primary care setting that are successfully billed and reimbursed under Collaborative Care Model (CoCM) CPT codes (99492, 99493, 99494) or screening codes (G0444, 96127).

## Why This Is THE Existential Metric

### The Structural Reality

Primary care provides **74% of antidepressant prescriptions** and handles **40% of mental health visits**. PCPs are doing the work. But without structured measurement and registry integration, they cannot legally bill the CoCM codes. They are leaving massive revenue on the table while margins compress.

### What Creates Urgency

| EDP Candidate | Urgency | Universality | Measurability | Actionability | Defensibility |
|--------------|---------|--------------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Clinician EHR Burnout Hours | 8/10 | 10/10 | 5/10 | 7/10 | 6/10 |
| Untreated High-Risk Patient Rate | 9/10 | 7/10 | 4/10 | 8/10 | 9/10 |
| **CoCM Revenue Realization Rate** | **10/10** | **8/10** | **9/10** | **10/10** | **8/10** |

**Why CoCM Revenue Realization wins:**
- **Measurable:** You can audit past claims data
- **Actionable:** Deploy Kivira to close the gap
- **CFO-level urgency:** Dollar amount, not soft metric
- **Defensible:** Harder for EHR incumbents to replicate full CoCM workflow automation

### The Math That Creates Urgency

If a health system has **10,000 patients with depression** but only bills CoCM codes for **500 of them** because their clinicians don't have time for structured screening and documentation, their Revenue Realization Rate is **5%**.

When Kivira enters and says: *"We automate the structured assessment, deliver the CDS directly into the Epic workflow, and auto-generate the documentation required for CPT 99492"* — Kivira pays for itself in the first 30 days.

## Contrast with Losing EDPs

### "Clinician Time Saved" (Why It Loses)
- "Saving time" is a soft metric
- Hard to guarantee in a contract
- Often fails to survive CFO value-analysis committees
- Strong, but not existential

### "Missed High-Risk Patients" (Why It Loses)
- Highly urgent and clinically critical
- But incredibly difficult to measure baseline data before Kivira is installed
- Great outcome metric, poor trigger for cold sale
- Defensible but not actionable on first call

## How to Use This EDP in GTM

### Permissionless Value Prop for CoCM Cap-Maxed Systems

> **Offer a "Lost Revenue Audit"**
>
> Ask for a blinded extract of their primary care depression diagnoses (ICD-10) versus their CoCM billed codes (CPT 99492). Show them the exact dollar amount they lost last quarter because their PCPs didn't have the EHR workflow to support the billing requirements.

### Permissionless Value Prop for VBC ACOs

> **Run a "Hidden Acuity" calculation**
>
> Show them national averages of how many severe depression cases are missed in standard 15-minute PCP visits, and calculate the expected ED utilization cost of those missed patients.

## Reimbursement Context

The CoCM codes **already exist**. The problem is that primary care clinics lack the structured assessment and decision-support workflows required to compliantly bill them.

From Bessemer: *"The most significant barrier to widespread clinical AI adoption isn't technology — it's payment models."*

But for CoCM specifically, the payment model exists. The barrier is workflow. Kivira solves workflow.

### Anticipated Catalyst: CMS AI CPT Codes (2026)

BVP predicts *"CMS will launch experiments to attempt to establish clinical AI payment codes"* in 2026. A CMS pilot creating explicit reimbursement for AI-assisted CDS would unlock a new class of health system buyer who today cannot justify the purchase on ROI grounds. Investors are pricing this optionality into valuations now.
