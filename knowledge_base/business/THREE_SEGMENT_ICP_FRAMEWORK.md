# Three-Segment ICP Framework

Kivira's GTM strategy targets three distinct buyer segments, each with different value propositions, sales motions, and timelines.

[INFERRED: Gemini directional analysis - verify before external use]

## Segment 1: Provider Group / VBC / PCP (Day 1 Focus)

**Profile:** Mid-market provider groups, value-based care practices, primary care clinics

**Sales Motion:** Email + Phone Call outreach for pilot programs

**Primary Value Prop:** [[DIRECT_BILLING_MODEL_FFS]]
- Immediate revenue via BHI billing codes
- "Self-funding" implementation
- Shortest sales cycle

**Why Day 1:**
- Fastest path to revenue
- Builds case studies for larger deals
- Validates product-market fit

## Segment 2: Health System / ACO / VBC Care Management

**Profile:** Larger enterprise health systems, ACOs, care management companies

**Sales Motion:** Enterprise sales, longer cycle

**Primary Value Prop:** [[MEDICAL_SPEND_MODEL_ER_AVOIDANCE]] + [[REVENUE_ACCURACY_MODEL_RISK_ADJUSTMENT]]
- Cost avoidance through ER reduction
- Risk adjustment accuracy
- Total cost of care improvements

**Why Later:**
- Longer sales cycle
- Requires case studies from Segment 1
- More complex integration requirements

## Segment 3: Payor / Health Plans

**Profile:** Medicare Advantage plans, commercial health plans, risk-bearing entities

**Sales Motion:** Enterprise/strategic partnerships

**Primary Value Prop:** [[REVENUE_ACCURACY_MODEL_RISK_ADJUSTMENT]]
- RAF score accuracy
- Star Rating improvements
- Medical Loss Ratio optimization

**Why Later:**
- Longest sales cycle
- Requires proven outcomes data
- Enterprise procurement complexity

## Execution Priority

Per Josh Pappas guidance:
1. **Day 1-30:** Build/test/warm up + dial PCPs (Segment 1)
2. **Day 30-60:** Measure results, signals
3. **Day 90+:** Results optimize, expand to Segments 2-3
