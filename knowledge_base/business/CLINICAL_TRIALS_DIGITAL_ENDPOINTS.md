# Clinical Trials Digital Endpoints

> **Status Note:** This is an expansion opportunity. Timing TBD - not prioritized in current execution.

The use of Kivira's digital phenotyping as a secondary endpoint in CNS clinical trials represents the "valuation play" - high margin, differentiated positioning.

[INFERRED: Gemini directional analysis - verify before external use]

## The Concept

Traditional psychiatric trials rely on periodic clinical interviews (e.g., monthly PHQ-9 or MADRS scores). Digital biomarkers provide continuous, objective data between visits.

**The shift:** From "Did the patient report fewer symptoms?" to "Did the patient's actual behavior change?"

## Where Kivira Fits in Trial Protocol

In a standard 12-week CNS trial:
1. **Pre-screening:** Objective baseline validation
2. **Week 0:** Digital baseline established
3. **Weekly:** Remote monitoring via patient app
4. **Endpoint:** Behavioral change data supports primary outcome

## FDA/EMA Considerations

Regulators have increased focus on Digital Biomarkers. Key requirements:
- 21 CFR Part 11 compliance (electronic records)
- Validation studies for the digital measures
- Clear relationship between digital and clinical endpoints

## Financial Logic

- **Per-study value:** Significant (varies by trial size and phase)
- **Protocol mandated:** If accepted, every site in a global trial must use the software
- **Data asset:** Builds longitudinal dataset valuable for Real-World Evidence (RWE)

## Why This Matters for Valuation

For Series A and beyond, Life Sciences revenue diversifies the customer base beyond healthcare providers. It also positions Kivira as "infrastructure" rather than just a clinical tool.
