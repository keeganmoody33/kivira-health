# Buyer Persona: The Operational Buyer

**Archetype:** Alex M. — Director of Behavioral Health Services / VP Population Health

[VERIFIED: Pattern synthesized from job postings, ContinuumCloud KPI benchmarks, SAMHSA toolkits, CMS billing guidance]

## Professional Snapshot

Master's degree in social work or public health, plus MBA earned while working. Career began in direct service as BH clinician, moved through program management, arrived at director/VP role after 8–12 years. Manages team including behavioral health care coordinators, psychiatric consultants (often contracted part-time), and operations staff across multiple clinic sites.

**Profile markers:**
- At systems scaling a CoCM program, her role is the operational engine that determines whether CoCM fidelity is maintained—and whether billing codes actually get submitted
- Reads ContinuumCloud KPI benchmarks, SAMHSA toolkits, CMS billing guidance booklets (MLN booklet, CPT 99492–99494), Peterson Health Technology Institute purchasing reports
- Increasingly active on LinkedIn posting about measurement-based care and CoCM scalability

**Real-world analog:** Sr. Medical Director for Behavioral Health Integration at a large regional health system (like Swedish Medical Center) who progressed from Behavioral Health Specialist → Clinical Program Director → Medical Director over 7–8 years within same organization. Builds programs from within rather than hopping between employers.

**Compensation:** $110,000–$180,000 (Director/VP level with clinical credentials)

## Daily Reality

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 8:00 AM | Reviews CoCM patient registry dashboard—how many patients in registry >10 weeks without improvement? How many overdue for psychiatric consultation? |
| 9:30 AM | Weekly check-in with BH care managers across five clinic sites—discussing caseload, documentation compliance, EHR workflow issues |
| 11:00 AM | Revenue cycle meeting—reconciling CoCM billing submissions for prior month; one site has high denial rate because care manager notes don't meet documentation requirements for 99492 |
| 1:00 PM | Vendor evaluation—comparing Kivira to NeuroFlow and one internal build option; built scoring matrix weighted toward ROI, documentation burden reduction, billing workflow compatibility |
| 3:00 PM | Quarterly program review for executive leadership—needs to show improvement in PHQ-9 follow-up rates and cost-per-patient metrics or risk losing headcount in next budget |
| 4:30 PM | Reviewing draft MOU for new psychiatric consultant relationship—coverage across three sites requires careful scheduling |

**Her KPIs:**
- CoCM patient registry size and active rate
- PHQ-9/GAD-7 completion and follow-up rates
- % patients with ≥50% symptom improvement (remission proxy)
- Appointments per care manager (productivity metric)
- CoCM billing capture rate (CPT 99492–99494) and denial rate
- No-show rate and time-to-access for new BH referrals
- Provider satisfaction with IBH workflow integration

**Billing is central:** Alex knows CoCM codes have measurable revenue implications—a well-run CoCM program can generate significant monthly reimbursement per enrolled patient, but only if documentation workflows are tight, time is tracked, and care manager is completing registry-based reviews. Any tool that disrupts this billing machinery loses her vote quickly. Any tool that tightens it wins a champion.

## Decision Driver Analysis

Alex is a **"show me the ROI" buyer**—but her ROI definition is multidimensional: clinical outcomes + operational efficiency + billing revenue capture, in approximately that order.

**Evaluation process:**
1. **Workflow compatibility audit:** Maps every step of current CoCM workflow against what tool requires—patient invitation, assessment completion, care manager review, clinician report delivery, escalation pathways. Any step that adds friction or requires retraining across 20+ staff is a risk flag.
2. **Billing integration check:** Will ask: "Does your tool help us capture more CoCM minutes? Does the documentation produced count toward 99492 time requirements?" If answer is ambiguous, escalates to revenue cycle partner.
3. **KPI alignment:** Will ask vendor to map demo output to her existing KPI dashboard. If tool cannot produce data she can actually use in quarterly report, it's not solving her real problem.
4. **Reference calls with operational peers:** Less interested in CMIO references; more interested in calling Director of Behavioral Health at another CoCM program. Will ask: "Did this reduce your care manager's documentation time? Did completion rates actually improve?"
5. **Contract structure scrutiny:** Aware that most digital health contracts run ≤2 years. Will negotiate for pilot-to-production gate with defined KPIs, because she knows CFO will ask at month 18 whether to renew.
6. **Equity and population fit:** Acutely aware that patient population may differ from validation cohort. Will ask about performance across demographic subgroups and whether assessment instruments have been validated in multilingual or low-literacy populations.

**What she needs from Kivira specifically:**
- How Kivira integrates with or supports the CoCM registry function
- Whether care manager time using the tool counts toward CoCM time requirements
- What the average patient completion rate is at live sites

## Communication Profile

**Preferred channels:** Email for formal proposals; phone or video for relationship-building; in-person site visits for complex multi-site rollouts

**Content she consumes:** SAMHSA PBHCI toolkits, AHRQ Integration Academy resources, CMS MLN booklets, AHA market scans, PHTI purchasing reports. Follows CFHA for practice benchmarks.

**Red flags:** Vendors who speak only to physician experience and not to care coordinator/care manager workflow; vendors who cannot answer billing compatibility questions; any tool requiring clinical informatics team to configure per-site (she doesn't control that budget)

**Meeting preference:** Working sessions that include her care manager supervisor—the person who will actually use the tool daily—not just executive-level pitches

**Response expectation:** Appreciates detailed written follow-ups she can share with CFO and clinical champion; bullets with data > narrative paragraphs

**Best engagement strategy:** Send one-page ROI summary anchored in CoCM billing math and care manager time savings, with reference to specific CPT codes in play

## Professional Motivation Map

**Career driver:** Wants to build a CoCM program that is operationally self-sustaining—one that doesn't require constant budget defense because it demonstrably pays for itself through billing revenue and quality bonuses. Longer-term aspiration: VP Population Health role shaping system-wide behavioral health strategy.

**Recognition currency:** Wants to present at SAMHSA national conference, be featured in AHRQ case study, or have program cited in Health Affairs as model for integrated care at scale. Also values internal recognition from CMO—being seen as person who "made integrated care actually work."

**Fear that shapes decisions:** A failed tool rollout that drains care manager time, frustrates frontline physicians, produces no measurable improvement—followed by CFO decision to cut BH integration budget. She has lived through one of those; will not let it happen again.

**Personal values:** Entered healthcare because she believes mental health access is a social justice issue. Motivated by statistic that only 6–10% of integrated primary care practices are located in highest-need communities. Wants Kivira (or any tool) to expand access, not just optimize it for already-well-resourced practices.

## Trigger to Yes

**Strongest proof point:** ROI one-pager with CoCM billing math and care manager time savings

**Fastest path to champion:** Show how tool maps to her quarterly KPI report

**Most common deal-killer:** No clear answer on CoCM billing compatibility
